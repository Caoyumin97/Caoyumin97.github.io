# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Tech professionals, researchers, and engineers who visit to read about AI agents, transportation ML research, and software engineering. Recruiters and collaborators evaluating background and taste. Fellow blog readers interested in the philosophy of AI-assisted development. The audience expects technical depth, clear thinking, and a distinct personal voice — not marketing fluff.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that presents Yumin Cao as a sharp, rigorous AI engineer with academic research roots. The site has two modes: the homepage delivers a SaaS-landing-page energy with glass effects and motion to establish credibility and personality; inner pages (blog, papers, projects) strip down to a more minimalist, reader-focused experience. The bilingual (EN/ZH) structure reflects a career bridging Chinese and international contexts.

## Brand Personality

sharp · rigorous · tech-like

Cooler than warm. Academic depth without academic stuffiness. Playful confidence in copy (see the 404 page: "An AI agent confidently assured me this page exists. It does not."). Visual design leans technical — serif typography for scholarly credibility, glassmorphism and blobs for a modern edge. Not trying to be everyone's friend; earns attention through substance.

## Anti-references

No hard anti-references. Homepage should draw from SaaS landing page energy (glass effects, interesting visuals, motion). Inner pages trend toward minimalist blog aesthetic. The two modes should feel visually unified rather than like separate sites.

## Design Principles

1. **Dual register, unified system.** The homepage can flex with glass, blur, and visual effects; inner pages pare down to reading-focused minimalism. The same color tokens, type system, spacing, and component vocabulary bind them together so the transition feels natural.

2. **Scholarly but not stuffy.** Serif typography (Fraunces, Literata, Noto Serif SC) anchors academic credibility. Visual effects, motion, and playful copy keep it from becoming a dry resume.

3. **Show, don't just tell.** Papers get full citations. Blog posts get tags and dates. Projects get tech stacks and GitHub links. The design surfaces substance without demanding clicks.

4. **Bilingual as a feature, not an afterthought.** Every content type supports parallel EN/ZH versions. Typography, line-height, and letter-spacing are tuned per language. The language toggle is always one click away.

5. **Motion with purpose.** Scroll reveals, staggered entry, blob drift — motion exists to guide attention and add atmosphere, not for decoration. Layout properties are never animated.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance (contrast, focus indicators, semantic HTML, keyboard navigation). No specific user group accommodations beyond standard AA requirements.
